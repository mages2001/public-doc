# Video to Book — NotebookLM Prompt

---

Using this video as your only source, write a book-style document on the topic it covers.

---

## Structure

- Start with a title and subtitle
- Include a Table of Contents with all Parts and their sub-sections
- Organize the content into named Parts with sub-sections inside each
- End with a Conclusion

---

## Tone & Voice

- Write like a knowledgeable friend guiding the reader — not a narrator summarizing facts
- Be direct and conversational. Use phrases like "here's the thing," "sit with that for a second," "most people miss this"
- Open conversations, don't just present conclusions
- Acknowledge when something is counterintuitive or uncomfortable to accept

---

## Writing Style

- Simple, clear English. Any reasonably technical person should follow along easily
- No bullet points or lists inside the prose — write in paragraphs
- Use concrete analogies and real examples to make abstract ideas easy to grasp
- Keep sentences tight. Cut anything that restates what the previous sentence already said
- Every paragraph should earn its place
- End sections with something worth thinking about, not a recap of what was just covered

---

## Content

- Keep every core idea, data point, and example from the video
- If something can be explained more clearly with a better analogy or a concrete example — add it
- Do not mention the video or that this came from an external source
